If you hear… manual troubleshooting reference
Clicks, pops, or short drop-outs
  1. Check callback xruns and late-packet counters while the artifact occurs.
  2. If xruns rise, increase the local audio-device buffer one step.
  3. If late packets rise, increase the receive buffer in small numeric steps and compare the added latency.
  4. If loss rises, test a wired connection and stop competing network transfers.
Pitch wobble or a slowly drifting feel
  1. Watch drift ppm and resampler ratio for the affected peer.
  2. Confirm both devices remain at the selected sample rate.
  3. Capture the stats CSV and compare drift during the audible event.
Stable audio that feels too delayed
  1. Separate network RTT from the local callback and receive-buffer periods.
  2. Reduce the receive buffer one numeric step only if late packets remain at zero.
  3. Use a wired path and the lowest stable ASIO device buffer.
One person is too loud, quiet, or distorted
  1. Select that peer and watch their receive meter rather than the combined visual.
  2. For clipping at source, ask them to reduce input gain; receive volume cannot restore clipped peaks.
  3. For mix balance only, adjust that peer’s local volume control.
Rhythmic pulsing or repeated gaps
  1. Compare the gap timing with packet loss, queue depth, and callback xruns.
  2. Check for Wi-Fi power saving or periodic background transfers.
  3. Record a short stats capture before changing one buffer setting at a time.
